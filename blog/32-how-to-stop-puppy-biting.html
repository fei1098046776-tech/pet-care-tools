How to Stop a Puppy from Biting and Nipping

Published: [DATE]

Puppy biting and nipping is a normal behavior. Puppies use their mouths to explore the world and play. However, their razor-sharp teeth can cause pain. Here is how to teach your puppy to stop nipping.

1. Bite Inhibition

Puppies learn bite inhibition from their mother and littermates. When a puppy bites too hard, the other puppy yelps and stops playing. You can mimic this: if your puppy nips you, make a high-pitched 'Ouch!' sound, limp your hand, and stop playing for 10-20 seconds.

2. Redirect to Toys

Always have a chew toy or teething toy handy. If your puppy tries to bite your hand or ankle, immediately place a toy in their mouth. Praise them when they chew the toy instead of you.

3. Avoid Physical Punishment

Hitting or squeezing a puppy's muzzle will make them fearful or aggressive. Consistency and redirection work much better than force.

4. Provide Teething Relief

Teething is painful. Frozen toys or wet washcloths can soothe their sore gums and reduce the urge to chew.
