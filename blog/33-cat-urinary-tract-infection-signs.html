Signs of Urinary Tract Infections (UTI) in Cats

Published: [DATE]

Urinary issues are common in cats and can quickly turn into life-threatening emergencies, especially for male cats. Recognizing the early signs of a Urinary Tract Infection (UTI) or blockage can save your cat's life.

Common UTI Symptoms

  • Straining to urinate: Spending a long time in the litter box while producing little to no urine.
  • Crying or howling: Vocalizing in pain while trying to urinate.
  • Urinating outside the box: Associating the litter box with pain, leading them to go on cool surfaces like tile or bathmats.
  • Blood in urine: Pinkish or bloody spots in the litter box.
  • Frequent licking: Licking their genital area to soothe discomfort.

When It is an Emergency

If your male cat is straining and unable to produce any urine, this is a blockage emergency. Urinary blockages can cause kidney failure and death within 24-48 hours. Seek emergency vet care immediately.
