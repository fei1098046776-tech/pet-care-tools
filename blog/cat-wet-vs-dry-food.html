The Ultimate Guide to Cat Nutrition: Wet vs Dry Food

Published: April 27, 2026 • 6 min read • Nutrition

One of the most fiercely debated topics among feline enthusiasts is the choice between wet food and dry kibble. While both can provide complete nutrition, understanding the benefits of each can help your cat live a longer, healthier life.

The Case for Wet Food (Canned)

Cats are obligate carnivores with a naturally low thirst drive. In the wild, they get most of their hydration from their prey. Wet food contains roughly 70-80% water, making it an excellent way to ensure your indoor cat stays hydrated. This significantly reduces the risk of urinary tract infections and kidney disease—two of the most common issues in older cats.

The Case for Dry Food (Kibble)

Dry food is undeniably convenient. It can be left out for free-feeding (though portion control is highly recommended to prevent obesity) without spoiling. It's also generally more cost-effective. However, dry food is dense in carbohydrates and calories.

Are You Overfeeding Your Cat?

Over 50% of domestic cats are overweight. Stop guessing and calculate exactly how many calories your cat needs per day.

The Best Approach: Combination Feeding

Many veterinarians now recommend a mixed diet. Offering wet food twice a day provides the necessary hydration and high protein, while leaving a measured amount of high-quality dry food allows for grazing.

Portion Control is Everything

Regardless of whether you choose wet, dry, or both, the total daily caloric intake is what matters most. An average 10lb indoor cat needs roughly 200-250 calories per day. Always check the kcal/cup or kcal/can on your pet food label and adjust accordingly.
