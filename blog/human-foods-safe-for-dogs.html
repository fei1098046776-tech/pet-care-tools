10 Human Foods That Are Completely Safe for Dogs

Published: April 27, 2026 • 5 min read • Diet

While we all know that chocolate and grapes are strictly off-limits, it doesn't mean your dog can't enjoy a tasty snack from the kitchen. Many human foods are not only safe but also packed with valuable nutrients.

The Safe List: Fruits & Veggies

  • Apples: An excellent source of vitamins A and C, plus fiber. Remember to remove the core and seeds first!
  • Carrots: Low in calories and high in fiber and beta-carotene. Crunching on carrots is also great for your dog's teeth.
  • Blueberries: Packed with antioxidants that prevent cell damage.
  • Watermelon: Made up of 92% water, making it a perfect hydrating treat on a hot summer day. Remove the rind and seeds.
  • Green Beans: Plain, unseasoned green beans are a fantastic low-calorie snack that keeps dogs feeling full.

The Safe List: Proteins & Carbs

  • Cooked Chicken: Unseasoned, boiled chicken is perfect when your dog has an upset stomach.
  • Peanut Butter: A classic favorite! Just ensure it is 100% natural and DOES NOT contain Xylitol (a deadly artificial sweetener).
  • Plain Yogurt: A good source of calcium and probiotics. Avoid yogurts with added sugars or artificial sweeteners.
  • White Rice: Easily digestible and great for settling stomach issues.
  • Pumpkin: Plain canned pumpkin (not pie filling!) works miracles for digestive health, curing both diarrhea and constipation.

Treats Have Calories Too!

Even healthy human foods contain calories. Treats should never make up more than 10% of your dog's daily diet.
