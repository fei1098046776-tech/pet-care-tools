Dangerous Foods Cats Should Never Eat: Toxic Food Guide

Published: [DATE]

Cats are curious animals and might try to steal food from your kitchen counter. However, many common human foods are highly toxic to cats and can cause organ failure or death. Here are the top foods to keep away from your feline friend.

1. Onions, Garlic, and Chives

All members of the onion family can cause red blood cell damage and anemia in cats. This applies to raw, cooked, and powdered forms.

2. Chocolate and Caffeine

Chocolate contains methylxanthines (specifically issues with theobromine), which can cause vomiting, diarrhea, high body temperature, muscle tremors, abnormal heart rhythm, and seizures.

3. Grapes and Raisins

Even small amounts of grapes or raisins can cause acute kidney failure in cats. The exact toxin is unknown, so it is best to avoid them entirely.

4. Alcohol and Raw Yeast

Yeast dough can expand in a cat's stomach, causing severe bloating and pain. Alcohol is highly toxic to their small livers and kidneys.

5. Xylitol (Artificial Sweetener)

Found in sugar-free candy and baked goods. It causes a rapid release of insulin, leading to hypoglycemia and liver failure.
