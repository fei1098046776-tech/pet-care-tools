7 Subtle Signs Your Indoor Cat Might Be Sick

Published: April 27, 2026 • 4 min read • Cat Health

Cats are evolutionary masters of disguise. In the wild, showing illness or pain makes a feline a target for predators. Unfortunately, our domestic house cats have retained this instinct, often hiding severe illnesses until they are critically sick.

Watch Out For These 7 Hidden Red Flags

  • 1. Changes in Litter Box Habits: Going outside the box, straining, or urinating more frequently are major warning signs of UTIs, kidney disease, or diabetes.
  • 2. Extreme Changes in Appetite: Eating significantly less is an obvious concern, but eating voraciously while losing weight is a classic sign of hyperthyroidism.
  • 3. Unkempt Coat: Cats are meticulous groomers. If their fur suddenly looks greasy, matted, or filled with dandruff, they likely don't feel well enough to clean themselves.
  • 4. Hiding More Than Usual: If your normally social cat suddenly spends all day under the bed or in the back of the closet, they might be in pain.
  • 5. Vocalization Changes: A quiet cat suddenly yowling at night, or a vocal cat going entirely silent.
  • 6. Bad Breath: Severe halitosis is often a sign of periodontal disease, which can lead to organ failure if the bacteria enters the bloodstream.
  • 7. Lethargy vs Normal Sleeping: Cats sleep a lot (12-16 hours a day). But if they don't wake up for their favorite treats or playtime, it's lethargy.

Is Your Cat's Weight Changing?

Sudden weight loss or gain is a primary indicator of illness. Keep their nutrition in check.
