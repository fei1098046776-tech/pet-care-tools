Cat Vaccination Schedule: A Complete Guide for Owners

Published: [DATE]

Vaccinations are critical to shielding your cat from preventable, life-threatening viral and bacterial infections. Staying on schedule ensures your kitten builds a strong immune foundation and your adult cat maintains immunity.

Kitten Core Vaccines (6-16 Weeks)

Kittens require a series of shots starting around 6 to 8 weeks of age, repeated every 3 to 4 weeks until they are about 16 weeks old. The primary core vaccine is FVRCP (Feline Viral Rhinotracheitis, Calicivirus, and Panleukopenia).

Rabies Vaccine

The Rabies vaccine is required by law in most areas. It is usually administered at 12 to 16 weeks of age, followed by a booster one year later, and then every 1 to 3 years depending on local regulations.

Non-Core Vaccines

FeLV (Feline Leukemia Virus) is highly recommended for cats that spend time outdoors or live in multi-cat households where exposure is possible.

Adult Booster Schedule

Once your cat reaches adulthood, booster shots are typically administered every 1 to 3 years depending on your vet's recommendations and your cat's lifestyle.
