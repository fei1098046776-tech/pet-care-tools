Why Do Cats Sleep So Much? Feline Sleep Habits Explained

Published: [DATE]

If you feel like your cat spends their entire life sleeping, you are not far off. The average cat sleeps between 12 and 16 hours a day, and some older cats sleep up to 20 hours! Here is the science behind their sleepy habits.

1. The Predator's Biology

Cats are natural-born predators. Hunting requires high bursts of energy, tracking, pouncing, and chasing. Sleeping allows them to conserve energy for the next hunt, even if their 'hunt' is just chasing a laser pointer.

2. Crepuscular Nature

Cats are crepuscular, meaning they are most active during twilight hours (dawn and dusk). This is when their natural prey is active. They sleep during the day and night to prepare for these active periods.

3. Do Cats Dream?

Yes! Cats experience both light sleep and deep REM (Rapid Eye Movement) sleep. During REM sleep, you might see their paws twitch or whiskers move, indicating they are dreaming of chasing mice.

4. Weather and Age

Cats will sleep more on rainy or cold days. Kittens and senior cats also require significantly more sleep than active adult cats.
