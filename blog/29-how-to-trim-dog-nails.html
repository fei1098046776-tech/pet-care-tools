How to Trim Your Dog's Nails Safely at Home

Published: [DATE]

Trimming your dog's nails is an essential part of grooming, but it can be nerve-wracking for both you and your pet. Long nails can cause discomfort, joint issues, and scratching. Here is how to trim them safely at home.

Choose the Right Tool

Use high-quality pet nail clippers (scissor-type or guillotine-type) or a nail grinder. Grinders are great for dogs that are fearful of clippers because they shave the nail down gradually.

Locating the Quick

The quick is the blood vessel that runs through the center of the nail. Cutting it causes bleeding and pain.

  • White/Light Nails: The quick is easily visible as a pink area inside the nail. Cut a few millimeters before it.
  • Black Nails: The quick is not visible. Clip very small slivers at a time. Stop when you see a black dot in the center of the cut nail.

What to Do if You Cut the Quick

Don't panic. Apply styptic powder or cornstarch to the nail tip with firm pressure to stop the bleeding within a few minutes.
