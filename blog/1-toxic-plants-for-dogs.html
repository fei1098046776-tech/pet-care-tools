5 Common Houseplants That Are Highly Toxic to Dogs

Emergency Care • Health Insights

Indoor plants add life and color to our homes, but as a pet owner, you must curate your indoor jungle carefully. Many incredibly common houseplants are highly poisonous to dogs and can cause severe medical emergencies if chewed or ingested.

1. Sago Palm (Extremely Toxic)

The Sago Palm is arguably the most dangerous plant you can keep around dogs. Every part of this plant is toxic, but the seeds are particularly deadly. Ingestion can cause acute liver failure and death within days. If you have a dog, do not keep a Sago Palm anywhere on your property.

2. Aloe Vera

While Aloe is a fantastic healing plant for humans, it's mild to moderately toxic for dogs. The gel inside the leaves is actually edible, but the outer leaf material contains saponins that cause lethargy, vomiting, and diarrhea in dogs.

3. Lily of the Valley

Beautiful but deadly. This flower contains cardiac glycosides, which can cause severe disruptions to a dog's heart rate, vomiting, and in severe cases, a coma. Even drinking water from a vase holding these flowers can be fatal.

4. Pothos (Devil's Ivy)

Pothos is one of the most popular, unkillable beginner houseplants. Unfortunately, it contains calcium oxalate crystals. When a dog bites a leaf, these crystals act like microscopic shards of glass, causing intense burning and irritation in the mouth, excessive drooling, and difficulty swallowing.

Has Your Dog Ingested Something Toxic?

While we calculate chocolate toxicity, plant poisoning requires immediate veterinary intervention. Call the ASPCA Animal Poison Control Center or your local emergency vet immediately.

5. Monstera Deliciosa

Like the Pothos, the trendy Monstera plant contains calcium oxalate crystals. While rarely fatal, it causes severe pain and swelling of the dog's lips, tongue, and throat.

The Rule of Thumb: Always research a plant's toxicity using reliable veterinary databases before bringing it into your home.
