The Ultimate Dog Vaccination Schedule: What Your Pup Really Needs

Health Insights • Puppy Care

Bringing home a new puppy is an incredibly exciting time, but navigating the world of veterinary medicine can be overwhelming. Ensuring your dog receives the correct vaccines at the right time is the most important step in giving them a long, healthy life.

Core vs. Non-Core Vaccines

Veterinarians divide vaccines into two categories. Core vaccines are considered absolutely vital to all dogs based on the risk of exposure, the severity of the disease, or transmissibility to humans. Non-core vaccines are lifestyle-dependent.

The Core Vaccines

  • Rabies: A fatal disease that is transmissible to humans. The rabies vaccine is legally required in almost all states and countries.
  • DAPP/DHPP: This is a combination vaccine that protects against four severe diseases: Distemper, Adenovirus (Hepatitis), Parainfluenza, and Parvovirus. Parvovirus is highly contagious and frequently fatal in young puppies.

Puppy Nutrition is Just as Important

Growing puppies require significantly more calories than adult dogs. Ensure they are getting enough fuel to build a strong immune system.

The Puppy Schedule (First Year)

Puppies receive antibodies from their mother's milk, but this protection wears off over time, which is why vaccines are administered in a staggered series.

  • 6-8 Weeks: First DAPP/DHPP shot.
  • 10-12 Weeks: Second DAPP/DHPP shot. (Non-core vaccines like Bordetella and Leptospirosis may be introduced here).
  • 14-16 Weeks: Third DAPP/DHPP shot. First Rabies shot.

Adult Booster Schedule

Once the initial puppy series is complete, dogs require a booster shot for DAPP and Rabies at 1 year of age. After this 1-year booster, modern veterinary guidelines recommend revaccinating for DAPP and Rabies every 3 years (though local rabies laws may vary).
