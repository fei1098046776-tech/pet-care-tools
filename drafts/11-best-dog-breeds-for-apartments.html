Best Dog Breeds for Apartment Living (2026 Guide)

Dog Care • Breeds • 7 min read

Living in an apartment doesn't mean you can't have a dog—it just means you need to choose the right one. The biggest mistake new dog owners make is assuming size is everything. A lazy Great Dane can be a better apartment dog than a hyperactive Jack Russell Terrier.

What Makes a Good Apartment Dog?

The key factors aren't size—they're energy level, noise, and adaptability. A good apartment dog typically:

  • Has a low-to-moderate energy level
  • Doesn't bark excessively (your neighbors will thank you)
  • Is comfortable being alone for reasonable periods
  • Adapts well to smaller spaces

🥇 1. French Bulldog

The undisputed king of apartment dogs. Frenchies are quiet, affectionate, and require minimal exercise—two short walks a day is plenty. They're also surprisingly good at sensing their owner's mood. The downside: they can be expensive and prone to breathing issues due to their flat faces.

🥈 2. Cavalier King Charles Spaniel

Gentle, quiet, and loving. Cavaliers are perfectly happy curling up on the couch all day and are excellent with children and other pets. They do require regular grooming due to their silky coat.

🥉 3. Bichon Frisé

Don't let the fluffy appearance fool you—Bichons are sturdy little dogs with big personalities. They're playful but not hyperactive, and their low-shedding coat makes them ideal for apartment living. They do need daily brushing to prevent matting.

4. Shih Tzu

Bred specifically to be indoor companions for Chinese royalty, Shih Tzus are perfectly engineered for apartment life. They love lounging on laps and are surprisingly calm for small dogs. Their exercise needs are minimal.

5. Greyhound (Yes, Really!)

Here's the surprise entry. Despite their size and racing reputation, retired greyhounds are famously known as "40mph couch potatoes." They need one good run a day but spend the rest of their time sleeping. They're also exceptionally quiet and gentle.

Breeds to Avoid in Apartments

Border Collies, Huskies, Dalmatians, and Weimaraners are working dogs that need hours of intense exercise daily. Without a yard and a job to do, they will become destructive. No amount of love can compensate for unmet exercise needs in these breeds.

The Bottom Line

Your lifestyle matters more than your square footage. If you're committed to two walks a day and plenty of mental stimulation, many dogs can thrive in apartments. Choose a breed whose energy level matches yours, not just one that looks cute.
