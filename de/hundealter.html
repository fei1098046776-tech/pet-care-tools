Why the "Multiply by 7" Rule is Wrong

For years, people believed that 1 dog year equals 7 human years. However, veterinary science shows that dogs mature extremely quickly in their first two years (a 1-year-old dog is essentially a 15-year-old teenager). After year two, the aging process slows down, but the rate depends heavily on the breed's size. Giant breeds age much faster in their later years compared to small breeds.
